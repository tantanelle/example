Defined Constants and Their Usage

Constants

  • PI: 3.14159 (Used in circle area calculation)
  • Gravity (m/s²): 9.8 (Used for force calculation)
  • Speed of Light (m/s): 299792458
  • Avogadro's Number: 6.02214076e+23
  • Boltzmann Constant (J/K): 1.380649e-23
  • Planck Constant (J·s): 6.62607015e-34
  • Earth Radius (km): 6371
  • Golden Ratio: 1.61803
  • Euler's Number: 2.71828
  • Atmospheric Pressure (Pa): 101325

Examples of Using Constants

Area of a circle with radius 10: 314.159 (using PI)

Force on a 50 kg object: 490 N (using Gravity)

Energy of a photon: 1.986445857e-25 J (using Planck's constant and speed of light)

Number of molecules in 0.002 mol: 1.204428152e+21 molecules (using Avogadro's number)

Energy at 300K: 4.141947e-21 J (using Boltzmann constant)

Earth's Circumference: 40030.1736 km (using Earth's radius)

Golden rectangle with width 5: 8.09015 (using Golden Ratio)

Natural logarithm of Euler's number: 1 (using Euler's number)

Force of air pressure on 0.05m²: 5066.25 N (using Atmospheric pressure)

Photon wavelength for frequency 5x10^14 Hz: 5.99584916e-07 meters (using speed of light)
